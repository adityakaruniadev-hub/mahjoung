## Critical Clarifying Questions

### 1. Mahjong Variant Market Analysis
**Question:** Which variant has the largest addressable market?

**Data:**
- **Japanese Riichi:** Strong global appeal (anime/manga influence), rule complexity = higher engagement, existing dominant player (Mahjong Soul)
- **Hong Kong Old Style:** Massive Chinese diaspora market, simpler rules = broader appeal, lower competition
- **Chinese Classical:** Huge domestic China market, but regulatory challenges (gambling laws, Great Firewall)
- **American Mahjong:** Niche, older demographic, limited growth potential

**Recommendation:** Japanese Riichi for global reach OR Hong Kong Old Style for underserved market.

### 2. Geographic Target
**Question:** Which regions should we prioritize?

**Options:**
- **Global:** Broadest reach, highest localization cost, varied regulations
- **Asia-first:** Largest existing market, high competition, potential gambling regulation issues
- **Western markets:** Growing interest, less competition, clearer regulations (if no gambling)
- **Chinese Diaspora:** Strong Mahjong culture, underserved by quality digital options

**Concerns:**
- China requires ICP license for hosting
- Japan has strict gambling laws affecting social casino features
- US/EU have clearer regulatory frameworks for skill games

### 4. Competitive Differentiation
**Question:** How will we compete against Mahjong Soul?

**Mahjong Soul Strengths:**
- Beautiful anime aesthetic
- Strong IP (collabs with anime franchises)
- Deep gameplay for serious players
- Large player base = fast matchmaking

**Potential Differentiation Angles:**

**Critical:** Cannot compete head-on with Mahjong Soul on their strengths. Need clear differentiation.

### 5. Monetization Strategy
**Question:** What model maximizes revenue while minimizing legal/regulatory risk?

**Options:**

**A. Pure Cosmetic (Mahjong Soul model)**
- Character avatars, table themes, tile designs, effects
- Pros: No gambling laws, broad appeal, sustainable
- Cons: Lower ARPU, requires large user base
- Estimated ARPU: $5-15/year

**B. Subscription Premium**
- Monthly fee for ranked play, tournaments, ad-free, exclusive cosmetics
- Pros: Predictable revenue, loyal user base
- Cons: Limits user base, requires strong value prop
- Estimated: $5-10/month

**C. Tournament Entry Fees**
- Pay to enter tournaments, winner takes pot
- Pros: High engagement, competitive appeal
- Cons: Gambling adjacent, legal complexity
- Risk: HIGH regulatory scrutiny

**D. Freemium with Progression**
- Virtual currency, unlocks, power-ups (but NOT pay-to-win)
- Pros: Familiar model, multiple revenue streams
- Cons: Can feel exploitative

**E. Ads-Supported**
- Video ads between games
- Pros: No payment friction
- Cons: Poor UX, low revenue per user

**My Recommendation:** Start with cosmetic monetization + optional subscription. Avoid gambling-adjacent models.

- Competitive games: $2-$10 CPI

**Strategy Recommendation:**

### 7. Retention Strategies

**Effective Strategies:**

- **Social features:** Friend lists, clubs, chat

### 8. Legal & Regulatory Landscape
**Question:** What are the regulatory concerns?

**Critical Issues:**

**Gambling Laws:**
- Any real money = gambling license required
- "Skill game" exceptions vary by jurisdiction
- Social casino models legal in most places but restricted advertising

**Data Privacy:**
- GDPR (EU) compliance required
- COPPA (US) if under-13 users possible
- China's PIPL if operating there

**Age Ratings:**
- Most app stores: 12+ or Teen for gambling themes
- Some regions stricter

**Recommendation:** Avoid any real money features. Position as "skill game" not gambling.
